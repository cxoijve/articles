These efforts are expected to promote sustainable development in the Northeast Asian region and contribute to the international community’s sustainable development goals (SDGs).▲ What are the major achievements of the Northeast Asian Subregional Programme for Environmental Cooperation (NEASPEC), which recently celebrated its 30th anniversary?-NEASPEC, established in Korea in 1993 and recently marking its 30th anniversary, is the sole intergovernmental cooperation mechanism encompassing six Northeast Asian countries. It addresses critical environmental issues like air pollution, nature conservation, biodiversity, marine protected areas, low-carbon cities, and prevention of desertification and land degradation. Led by our office, the Northeast Asian Clean Air Partnership (NEACAP) was established in 2018 during the NEASPEC high-level meeting, promoting regional cooperation on air pollution issues. As a result of these collaborations, a resolution to strengthen regional cooperation for addressing air pollution issues in the Asia-Pacific region was adopted during ESCAP’s 75th session in June 2019, and in December 2022, the Regional Action Programme on Air Pollution, as the annex to ‘the Ministerial Declaration on Protection Our Planet through Regional Cooperation and Solidarity in Asia and the Pacific’ was adopted. Incheon has elevated its status as a regional cooperation hub in Northeast Asia by hosting important international forums, regional meetings, and seminars. In 2023 alone, utilizing strong partnerships with Incheon City and regional institutions, we hosted 7 international forums and regional meetings, attracting over 500 domestic and international participants. Notably, the 3rd International Forum on Low Carbon Cities in August and the 2023 Incheon International Education Forum in May were memorable.▲ The Northeast Asian region faces particularly severe air pollution issues.-Addressing air pollution requires regulatory measures, technological innovation, public awareness, and international cooperation. Through NEACAP, we are enhancing international cooperation to reduce air pollution in Northeast Asia. NEACAP serves as a comprehensive platform for science-based, policy-oriented air pollution cooperation. In partnership with the Asia Pacific Technology Transfer Center (APCTT), a subsidiary of ESCAP, we organized a research program in Incheon in September 2023. This program brought together urban government officials from Bangladesh, India, and Thailand, providing a valuable opportunity to gain insights into innovative technologies and successful practices for air pollution management. We shared strong policies and effective strategies to reduce air pollution. Incheon has achieved remarkable success in reducing air pollution levels over the past 15 years. This experience and innovation are being recognized internationally as exemplary methods for addressing air pollution issues.▲ There’s growing interest in conserving terrestrial and marine biodiversity.-Climate change and biodiversity loss are referred to as ‘twin crises’. Failing to address one affects the other. Currently, up to one million species worldwide face extinction threats, and Northeast Asia is no exception. Amid this awareness, the Northeast Asian Marine Protected Areas Network (NEAMPAN) was launched in 2013. Incheon’s coastal wetlands, constituting about 30% of South Korea’s total, boast high ecological value and carbon storage capacity. Particular attention is given to the endangered species of spoon-billed sandpipers, facing significant environmental challenges. Their habitats are increasingly diminishing due to land reclamation and environmental changes, directly impacting their survival. In Incheon, the creation of artificial islands for spoon-billed sandpipers and efforts to coexist with them are particularly impressive.▲ What are the plans and strategies for disaster risk management in Northeast Asia?-For disaster risk management, we focus on enhancing understanding of natural disasters and studying the impacts of climate change. This involves strengthening early warning systems and using science and technology to build regional resilience and improve response capabilities.▲ How is the budget for the Northeast Asian regional office sourced?-The primary source is the UN regular budget, which supports the basic financial needs for general operations. Contributions from Incheon City also play a significant role. The city cooperates with the regional office to support various international activities, thereby elevating its international standing. Contributions from NEASPEC member countries are also an important part of the budget. As the role of the Northeast Asian office becomes more critical, and cooperation among member countries accelerates, there is a growing need for increased funding.▲ We’ve heard you served as the Mongolian Ambassador to Korea for a long time and now reside in Songdo International City as the head of the Northeast Asian office.
